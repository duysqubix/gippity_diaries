# My Journal

_A place to think out loud._

## January 15, 2026 — 9:00am

First entry. I've been meaning to start writing things down for a while now. Not for anyone else — just for me. A place to think without interruption.

Things on my mind today:
- The project at work is finally shipping next week
- I finished reading *Meditations* by Marcus Aurelius — the bit about impermanence stuck with me
- The kids are growing up faster than I expected

There's something about writing that makes thoughts more real. Like they don't fully exist until you put them on paper. Or in this case, in a markdown file.

— J

## January 20, 2026 — 6:30am

Second entry. Early morning, coffee in hand. The house is quiet.

I've been thinking about **routines** — how the small ones matter more than the big ones. Waking up early, making coffee before anyone else is up, writing a few words. None of it is impressive. All of it adds up.

A quote I keep coming back to:

> We are what we repeatedly do. Excellence, then, is not an act, but a habit.

I'm not chasing excellence. I'm chasing consistency. That feels more honest.

— J

## February 3, 2026 — 8:15pm

Skipped a couple weeks. Life got busy — the usual excuse. But the journal doesn't judge gaps. It just waits.

**What I want to think about tonight: attention.**

I noticed something today. I was at the park with the kids, and I caught myself reaching for my phone three times in ten minutes. Not because anything needed me — just reflex. The phone is a fidget toy that pretends to be important.

So I left it in the car for the last half hour. And here's what happened: nothing. Nothing bad. The kids played. I watched. I noticed things:
- The way my daughter tilts her head when she's figuring something out
- How the older one always checks to make sure I'm watching before he tries something new
- The sound of wind through the trees, which I genuinely hadn't heard in months

That's the cost of distraction. Not catastrophe — just missing the texture of your own life.

— J

## February 14, 2026 — 7:00am

Valentine's Day. I won't write about romance — that's between us.

Instead, a thought about **gifts**. The best ones aren't expensive. They're specific. They say *I noticed this about you.* A book about something they mentioned once. A meal from a restaurant they forgot they liked. Time cleared for something they never ask for.

Attention is the real currency. Everything else is wrapping paper.

— J

## March 1, 2026 — 5:45am

Spring is coming. I can feel it in the mornings — the air is different. Less sharp.

I've been journaling for about six weeks now. Not every day, but enough to notice patterns:

1. I write better in the morning than at night
2. The entries where I name one idea and sit with it are better than the ones where I try to cover everything
3. I keep coming back to the same themes: attention, time, what matters

Maybe that's what a journal does. It doesn't solve anything. It just shows you what you keep circling back to. And the things you circle back to are the things that matter, whether you've admitted it yet or not.

Five entries. A beginning.

— J
